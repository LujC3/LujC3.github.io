Portfolio

Four projects. One consistent finding.

📌
Across four independent NZ government datasets, the same pattern emerges: Pacific and Māori communities face systemic disadvantage in health, housing, income, and education. These projects use data to make that case with evidence.
↗
01 — Health
NZ Health Waitlist Analysis
A decade of Te Whatu Ora FSA waitlist data (44,735 rows) analysed by region, specialty, and ethnicity. Covers 2015–2025.
Pacific patients wait at 45.9% vs European at 34.2% — an 11.7 percentage point equity gap.
Python pandas matplotlib Jupyter
↗
02 — Governance
NZ Government Procurement Transparency
$14.9 billion in MBIE GETS contract data (2019–2025) analysed for supplier concentration and competition rates.
Fulton Hogan appears under 4 name variants — combined 108 contracts, masking true market concentration.
Python SQL SQLite pandas
↗
03 — Income & Housing
NZ Census Income & Housing Inequality
2023 Census data combining median personal income and housing tenure rates by ethnicity across New Zealand.
Pacific Peoples: $35,200 income, 30.1% home ownership — vs European $42,700 and 59.3%.
Python pandas Stats NZ matplotlib
↗
04 — Education
NZ Education Inequality Analysis
Highest qualification by ethnicity from the 2013, 2018, and 2023 Censuses — tracking whether education gaps are closing.
22% of Pacific Peoples hold no qualification vs 9.3% of Asian communities. The gap is not closing.
Python pandas matplotlib Census 2023
